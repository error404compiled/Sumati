There is a particular quality of light just before sunrise — soft, hesitant, as
if the day itself is taking a slow breath before it begins. I stood at the edge
of the hill this morning and watched the horizon blush into gold, and the
spectacle before us was indeed sublime.

## Awe is a doorway

We often chase extraordinary experiences, believing that wonder lives somewhere
far away. Yet awe is rarely about distance. It is about attention. The same
sunrise has greeted this hill for millions of mornings; what changed today was
simply that I stopped to notice.

> Wonder is the seed. Attention is the soil.

When we slow down enough to truly see, the ordinary reveals itself as
extraordinary. A cup of tea steaming in the cold. The laughter of someone we
love. The quiet loyalty of breath, rising and falling without our permission.

## Three gentle practices

1. **Pause at thresholds.** Before entering a room or beginning a task, take one
   conscious breath.
2. **Name one beautiful thing.** Each evening, recall a single moment that moved
   you.
3. **Let go of the verdict.** Notice without judging whether the moment was
   "good" or "bad." Simply let it be.

May you find something sublime today — not in the distance, but right here, in
the small and sacred ordinary.
