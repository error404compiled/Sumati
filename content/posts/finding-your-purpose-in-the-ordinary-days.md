We are told that purpose arrives like lightning — a single, dazzling moment of
clarity that reorganizes our entire life. But for most of us, purpose is not a
thunderclap. It is a quiet thread, woven through the ordinary days.

## Purpose is built, not found

We imagine purpose as something hidden, waiting to be discovered like treasure.
But more often, purpose is *built* — assembled slowly through the small choices
of how we spend our attention, our love, and our hours.

> You do not find your purpose. You practice it into being.

## Follow what gives you life

Pay attention to the moments when you feel most alive — when time disappears and
you forget to check the clock. Those moments are clues. Follow them gently,
without pressure. Purpose is less a destination and more a direction.

Show up. Stay curious. Serve where you can. The thread will reveal itself, one
ordinary day at a time.
