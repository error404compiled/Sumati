Far far away, behind the word mountains, far from the countries of constant
notifications and never-ending to-do lists, there lives a quieter version of
you. Not a more productive you. Not a more impressive you. Simply a more
*present* one.

## The noise we carry

We tend to imagine peace as a place — a beach, a retreat, a mountain cabin. But
the truth is gentler and more inconvenient: peace is a practice we carry within
us. We can travel to the most serene corner of the earth and still bring the
storm of our thoughts along.

## Coming home to stillness

Stillness is not the absence of activity. It is the presence of attention. You
can find it while washing dishes, walking to work, or waiting in line. The
invitation is always the same — return, gently, to this breath, this moment,
this life.

- Breathe in for four counts.
- Hold for four.
- Release for six.
- Notice that you are still here.

The quieter self was never lost. It was only waiting, patiently, behind the word
mountains, for you to come home.
