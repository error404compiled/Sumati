Gratitude is not about pretending everything is perfect. It is about noticing
what is good, even while we long for what is better. It is the quiet practice of
saying *thank you* before the day demands anything of us.

## What gratitude really is

Gratitude is not naïve. It does not deny pain or difficulty. Instead, it widens
our vision so that suffering is not the only thing we can see. A grateful heart
holds both the ache and the blessing at once.

## A simple evening ritual

Before you sleep tonight, name three things:

1. Something that made you smile.
2. Someone who helped you.
3. Something about yourself you are thankful for.

Do this for a week, and you will begin to notice your mind reaching, on its own,
for the good. Gratitude is a seed — plant it daily, and joy will grow where you
least expect it.
